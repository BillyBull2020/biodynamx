# Google My Business (GBP) Optimization Skill

## When to Apply This Skill

- When building any local business tool or onboarding flow
- When a client or partner needs to set up their Google Business Profile
- When optimizing for local SEO, Google Maps, or voice search ("near me" queries)
- When creating business directories, landing pages, or audit tools
- When training AI agents on local business optimization

## The 12-Point GBP Setup Framework

### 1. Business Name (Critical)

- Use your **exact legal business name** — no keyword stuffing
- ❌ Wrong: "BioDynamX AI — Best AI Receptionist Voice Agent Automation"
- ✅ Right: "BioDynamX Engineering Group"
- Google penalizes keyword-stuffed names with suspension

### 2. Primary Category Selection (Critical)

- Choose the **most specific** primary category that matches your core service
- Add 5-9 **secondary categories** for related services
- Example for BioDynamX: Primary = "Software Company", Secondary = "Business Management Consultant", "Marketing Agency", "Technology Company"
- Research competitors' categories using tools like PlePer or GMB Spy

### 3. Business Description (500-750 chars)

- First 250 characters are most important (shown in preview)
- Include: What you do, who you serve, what makes you different, service area
- Include 3-5 natural keyword phrases
- End with a soft CTA
- Template:

  ```
  [Company Name] is [what you do] for [who you serve]. We specialize in [core services] using [differentiator]. Serving [service area], we help businesses [key outcome]. [Unique selling point]. [CTA — "Contact us for a free consultation."]
  ```

### 4. Contact Information

- **Phone**: Use a local number (not toll-free) if possible — local numbers rank better in local pack
- **Website**: Link to homepage with UTM tracking: `?utm_source=google&utm_medium=organic&utm_campaign=gbp`
- **Appointment URL**: Link directly to booking/audit page
- Keep NAP (Name, Address, Phone) consistent across ALL directories

### 5. Address & Service Area

- **Physical location**: If you have one, use it. Physical locations rank higher.
- **Service-area business**: If no physical location, set service areas by city/region (up to 20)
- **Hybrid**: If you serve customers at your location AND travel to them, use both
- Hide address if you don't want walk-ins (home office, etc.)

### 6. Business Hours

- Set **accurate** hours — Google cross-references with activity signals
- Add **special hours** for holidays BEFORE they happen
- If 24/7 (like AI services), list as 24 hours — this helps voice search: "Who's open now?"
- Add "More hours" for specific services if applicable

### 7. Photos & Visual Content (15+ minimum)

Required photos:

- **Cover photo**: 1024x576px, shows your brand/storefront
- **Logo**: 720x720px, square, clean
- **Interior photos**: 3-5 shots showing workspace/office
- **Team photos**: 3-5 shots of team members
- **Product/service photos**: 5+ showing your work/results
- **Customer interaction photos**: Shows social proof
- Upload new photos weekly — businesses with 100+ photos get 520% more calls
- Geotag photos with your business coordinates before uploading

### 8. Google Posts (Weekly)

Post types to rotate:

- **What's New**: Company updates, new features, milestones
- **Offers**: Promotions, free trials, limited-time deals
- **Events**: Webinars, workshops, launch events
- **Products**: Highlight specific services/products
- Template:

  ```
  🔥 [Attention-grabbing headline]
  
  [2-3 sentences about the value/offer]
  
  [CTA with link]
  ```

- Post at least 1x/week — Google rewards active profiles
- Include a CTA button on every post

### 9. Products & Services Section

- List EVERY service with:
  - Name
  - Description (unique, keyword-rich, 150-300 chars)
  - Price or price range
  - Link to relevant page on your website
- Google uses this data for "service near me" queries

### 10. Reviews Strategy (Most Important Ranking Factor)

- **Target**: 50+ reviews in first 90 days, then 5+/month ongoing
- **Ask timing**: 24-48 hours after positive experience (dopamine is still active)
- **Review generation tactics**:
  - Send direct review link via SMS after service: `https://g.page/[your-business]/review`
  - Add QR code to receipts, invoices, follow-up emails
  - Train staff: "Would you mind leaving us a quick Google review? It really helps."
- **Respond to EVERY review** within 24 hours:
  - Positive: Thank + mention specific service + invite back
  - Negative: Apologize + take offline + show resolution
- **Never** buy reviews, incentivize with discounts, or review-gate

### 11. Q&A Section (Proactive)

- **Seed your own Q&A** — ask and answer the top 10 questions customers have
- This feeds directly into Google's AI Overviews and voice search
- Example questions to seed:
  1. "What services does [Business] offer?"
  2. "What are [Business]'s hours?"
  3. "Does [Business] offer free consultations?"
  4. "How much does [core service] cost?"
  5. "What areas does [Business] serve?"
- Answer each with 100-200 words, keyword-rich

### 12. Attributes & Special Features

- Enable all applicable attributes:
  - Online appointments
  - Online estimates
  - Languages spoken
  - Accessibility features
  - Payment methods
  - Health & safety measures
- These power Google's filter-based searches

## Monthly Maintenance Checklist

- [ ] Post 4+ Google Posts (1/week minimum)
- [ ] Upload 5+ new photos
- [ ] Respond to all new reviews within 24 hours
- [ ] Update any changed hours/services
- [ ] Check for and answer new Q&A
- [ ] Monitor insights: views, searches, actions
- [ ] Check for Google-suggested edits and approve/reject

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. ❌ Keyword stuffing the business name (causes suspension)
2. ❌ Using a virtual office address (Google catches this)
3. ❌ Inconsistent NAP across directories (kills rankings)
4. ❌ Not responding to reviews (signals abandonment)
5. ❌ Using stock photos (Google's AI detects them)
6. ❌ Setting and forgetting (requires weekly maintenance)
7. ❌ Ignoring the Q&A section (leaving it empty)
8. ❌ Not tracking insights (can't improve what you don't measure)
