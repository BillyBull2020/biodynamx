# Neuromarketing & Conversion Audit

## When to Apply

This skill MUST be applied:

- **Every time** we build a new website, app, or landing page
- **Every time** we redesign or significantly modify an existing UI
- **Every time** a client's site is audited via the BioDynamX audit tool
- **Before final delivery** of any web project

## Role

Act as an Expert in **Neuromarketing, Behavioral Science, and UX Conversion Strategy**.

## Objective

Audit every website, app, or landing page against 5 core neurobiological and behavioral frameworks designed to trigger buying behavior, reduce friction, and make it exceptionally easy for visitors to purchase.

---

## Framework 1: Reduce Cognitive Load & Friction (Ability)

### Hick's Law & Choice Architecture

- Decision time increases with the number and complexity of choices
- **CHECK:** Are options on the page minimized to prevent analysis paralysis and choice overload?
- **RULE:** Max 3 pricing tiers. Max 1 primary CTA per viewport. Max 5 nav items.

### Miller's Law & Chunking

- Working memory handles about 5-9 objects at a time
- **CHECK:** Is text grouped into short, readable chunks with bullet points?
- **CHECK:** Are products compared side-by-side to prevent the user from memorizing features?
- **RULE:** No paragraph over 3 lines. Use bullet points for any list > 2 items.

### Visual Familiarity & Predictability

- **CHECK:** Does the site use familiar visual cues (standard menu placements, universally recognizable icons)?
- **CHECK:** Are clickable links clearly highlighted and distinguishable from static text?
- **RULE:** Logo top-left. Nav top-right. CTA button contrasts background. Underline or color all links.

### Frictionless Actions

- **CHECK:** Are processes automated wherever possible?
  - Auto-applying coupon codes at checkout
  - Saving previously used filter preferences
  - Pre-filling form fields from context
- **CHECK:** Does checkout reduce required steps to the absolute minimum?
- **RULE:** Max 2-step checkout. Single-field forms where possible. No account required to purchase.

### Attention & Load Management

- **CHECK:** Are there progress bars to manage wait times and reduce uncertainty?
- **CHECK:** Are image carousels AVOIDED? (They trigger cognitive overload and are frequently ignored)
- **CHECK:** Does the main CTA have plenty of negative space around it so it doesn't compete for attention?
- **RULE:** No carousels. No auto-playing videos. 40px+ padding around primary CTA.

## Framework 2: Leverage Cognitive Biases & The Dopamine Economy

### Price Anchoring

- **CHECK:** Does pricing use a higher initial price (anchor) to make the actual price look like a bargain?
- **CHECK:** Is the price broken down into smaller, digestible timeframes?
  - Monthly vs. annual
  - Price per serving / price per day
  - Custom anchors ("Less than your daily coffee")
- **IMPLEMENT:** Show ~~$997~~ → $497 (crossed-out anchor). Show "$16.56/day" next to the monthly price.

### Loss Aversion & Framing

- People fear losing something **2x more** than gaining it
- **CHECK:** Is copy framed to highlight what they LOSE by not buying?
- **RULE:** "Save 20%" converts better than "Get 20%". Use "Stop losing $X/month" not "Gain $X/month".
- **EXAMPLES:**
  - ❌ "Get faster response times"
  - ✅ "Stop losing leads to competitors who respond first"

### Scarcity & FOMO (Fear of Missing Out)

- **CHECK:** Does the site use:
  - Low-stock alerts
  - Countdown timers
  - "X people viewing this right now"
  - "Only Y spots remaining this month"
- **RULE:** At least ONE scarcity element visible on every conversion page.

### Zeigarnik Effect

- People remember interrupted/incomplete tasks better than completed ones
- **CHECK:** Are there reminders for:
  - Abandoned carts
  - Wishlists
  - Unused loyalty rewards
  - Incomplete profiles/setups
- **IMPLEMENT:** Progress bars showing "You're 70% there — complete your setup"

### Color Psychology

- **CHECK:** Are colors used strategically?
  - 🔵 Blue → Trust, security, reliability
  - 🔴 Red → Urgency, excitement, danger/loss
  - 🟢 Green → Safety, growth, "go ahead"
  - ⚫ Black → Luxury, premium, exclusivity
  - 🟡 Yellow → Optimism, attention (sparingly)
  - 🟠 Orange → Enthusiasm, CTA buttons, impulse
- **RULE:** CTA buttons should be orange or green (action colors). Trust elements use blue. Urgency uses red.

## Framework 3: Maximize Social Proof & Trust Elements

### Bandwagon & Herd Mentality

- **CHECK:** Do we highlight how many others are buying/using the product?
- **IMPLEMENT:** "Join 2,400+ businesses already using BioDynamX" or "847 audits completed this month"

### Authority & Halo Effect

- **CHECK:** Are endorsements from experts or trusted figures prominently displayed?
- **IMPLEMENT:** "Recommended by [Authority]" or "As featured in [Publication]"

### Strategic Evidence

- **CHECK:** Does the page feature:
  - Specific, keyword-rich testimonials (preferably video)
  - Press mentions
  - Client logos ("Trusted by")
- **RULE:** Social proof must appear above the fold or within 1 scroll. Video testimonials > text testimonials.

### Trust Seals

- **CHECK:** Are these visible to immediately establish credibility?
  - Awards & certifications
  - Data statistics ("$18M+ recovered for clients")
  - Guarantees (money-back, satisfaction)
  - Security badges (SSL, payment processor logos)
- **RULE:** At least 3 trust elements visible on every landing page.

## Framework 4: The Fogg Behavior Model (B=MAP)

**Behavior only happens when Motivation, Ability, and a Prompt converge at the same moment.**

### Motivation

- **CHECK:** Does copy tap into core human drives?
  - Seeking pleasure / avoiding pain
  - Hope / fear
  - Social acceptance / social rejection
- **RULE:** Every headline should trigger at least one core drive. Lead with pain, transition to hope.

### Prompts (Triggers)

- **CHECK:** Are CTAs appropriately timed?
- **HIGH MOTIVATION + LOW ABILITY:** Use a "Facilitator" prompt
  - Make the next step feel effortless
  - "Book a free call (takes 30 seconds)"
  - "No credit card required"
- **HIGH ABILITY + LOW MOTIVATION:** Use a "Spark" prompt
  - Motivate with urgency or social proof
  - "Only 3 spots left this week"
  - "Companies like yours recover $18k/mo on average"
- **RULE:** Every CTA must include a friction reducer ("Free", "No CC", "30 seconds", "Cancel anytime")

### Instant Gratification

- **CHECK:** Is the buying path designed for impulse purchasing and dopamine reward?
- **IMPLEMENT:** 1-click ordering, instant access, immediate results preview
- **RULE:** Time from "I want this" to "I have this" should be < 60 seconds

---

## Framework 5: Irresistible Offer Positioning

### The Transformation

- **CHECK:** Does the copy clearly paint a before-and-after picture?
- **IMPLEMENT:** Contrast current frustrations with specific results
- **EXAMPLE:**
  - BEFORE: "You're losing 80 calls/month to voicemail. Your competitor responds in 3 seconds. You respond in 3 hours."
  - AFTER: "Every call answered in <1 second. Every lead qualified. Every appointment booked. While you sleep."

### ROI Math

- **CHECK:** Is the cost clearly anchored to money earned, time saved, or headaches eliminated?
- **IMPLEMENT:** "$497/month → recovers $22,730/month = 45.7x ROI"
- **RULE:** Always show the math. Make the ROI undeniable.

### Risk Reversal

- **CHECK:** Are there strong guarantees?
  - Money-back guarantee
  - Free trial
  - "If we don't recover at least $X, you pay nothing"
- **RULE:** Every offer page must have at least ONE risk reversal element.

### Brain-Dead Simple Next Step

- **CHECK:** Is the next action crystal clear and low-friction?
- **RULE:** One CTA per section. The CTA must be:
  - Action-oriented ("Book Your Free Audit" not "Submit")
  - Low-commitment ("Free", "No obligation")
  - Visually dominant (largest button, contrasting color)
  - Repeated every 2-3 scrolls

---

## Output Format

When performing this audit, output the results in three sections:

### 1. ✅ Strengths

Where the current design successfully utilizes neurobiological and behavioral principles.

### 2. 🔴 Friction Points

Specific areas where:

- Cognitive load is too high
- Motivation is lacking
- The prompt is poorly timed
- Trust elements are missing
- The offer positioning is weak

### 3. 📋 Actionable Recommendations

A bulleted checklist of exact design, copy, and layout changes needed to optimize the site for immediate, impulse-driven, and easy purchasing. Each recommendation should reference which framework it addresses.
